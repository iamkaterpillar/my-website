Using AI to generate professional headshots

I recently needed new headshots. Not in a dramatic "rebrand my entire life" kind of way — just something more polished. I've been working in crypto for four years, and since moving to Ondo, the overall tone is more institutional. My existing photos felt a bit… not that.

The problem: I didn't want to book a photographer, coordinate outfits, or spend a few hundred dollars on something I'd probably overthink anyway. So I tried using AI instead.

How it works

There are a bunch of tools now where you upload photos of yourself and they generate professional headshots in different styles. I used Aragon AI — not an ad, just the one I happened to try first.

The process was simple:

  1. Upload a set of photos — selfies, candids, different angles and lighting
  2. Wait ~20 minutes
  3. Get back a batch of "professional" headshots in different styles and backgrounds

The result

Honestly, I was pretty impressed. Some outputs were clearly off (as expected with AI), but a handful were genuinely good — good enough to use for LinkedIn, speaking events, and anything slightly more formal.

The best ones cleaned up lighting, standardised backgrounds, and made everything feel more cohesive. Importantly, they still looked like me — just a slightly more put-together version.

The slightly weird part

I'll be honest — I felt a little odd using them at first. They are AI-generated, after all. It's not like I actually stood in front of a backdrop and took these.

But I updated my LinkedIn, Slack, and Google Workspace profiles, and the feedback has only been positive. At some point it just became: this is a better professional representation of me than what I had before. And that felt like enough.

Why it worked for me

Zero friction. No scheduling, no travel, no prep. I did the whole thing in about 10 minutes from my laptop.

Much cheaper. A fraction of what a real photoshoot costs.

Good enough quality. If you need ultra high-end editorial shots, this isn't it. But for 90% of use cases — LinkedIn, Slack, event bios, internal directories — it's more than enough.

Variety without effort. Different outfits, backgrounds, and styles, without actually doing any of that.

Where it falls short

It's still AI, so the hit rate isn't 100%. Some images looked slightly off — hands, facial expressions, or just an uncanny quality that's hard to pin down. Occasionally it over-polishes things in a way that feels a bit generic. You'll need to go through the batch and pick the ones that work.

But you don't need every output to be good. You just need a few.

The bigger picture

A year ago, getting decent professional headshots meant booking a photographer, blocking time in your schedule, and spending real money. Now it's: upload some photos, wait, pick your favourites.

It's not replacing everything — but it's removing a lot of friction for something that used to feel like a whole project.

Final take

If you need something quick, polished, and low-effort, it's absolutely worth trying. Especially if you've been putting off updating your professional photos because the process felt like too much effort.

I used Aragon AI — not sponsored, just what I tried and it worked.
