Claude CoWork is changing the game

I've been using AI tools for a while now, but Claude CoWork hit differently. It's not just a better chatbot — it's closer to having a capable colleague who can actually take tasks off your plate, connect to the tools you already use, and do things that would have taken hours in minutes.

Here are three ways I've used it recently.

1. Dune analytics — without a Dune wizard

We have some great team Dune dashboards tracking Ondo's onchain activity across partners. But I wanted to go deeper — things like which tokens are trending by chain, week-over-week TVL growth, what people are buying on ETH vs BNB vs Solana, and how we compare to xStocks (one of our main competitors) across the tokens we've both tokenized.

With the Dune MCP, I was able to build all of that myself.

It took a few hours of back-and-forth to get the queries right and debug some edge cases, but I got there. The dashboard is now genuinely useful for my day-to-day analytics — the kind of work we'd have previously hired a Dune wizard for and spent significantly more on.

I'm also using it for monitoring. I track when Ondo hits major TVL milestones — and instead of refreshing a dashboard obsessively, I've set up an automated check: Claude monitors the TVL and pings me on Slack the moment we hit the next target. That one alone has saved me a lot of anxious tab-switching.

One real downside: I burned through all my Dune credits in a single day. Worth knowing if you're planning to go deep on analytics.

2. Slides in 10 minutes instead of 2 hours

I needed to put together a few slides. I had all the data — I just wasn't in the headspace to wrangle it into a deck. Formatting, making it look good, getting the numbers in the right place. All the annoying parts.

So I tried something different. I gave Claude CoWork a rough outline of what I wanted on each slide, dropped in the spreadsheets with the data, and shared a template deck for design reference — logos, colours, layout style.

Ten minutes later I had a solid draft. What would have taken me one to two hours was done before I'd finished my coffee. The formatting was clean, the data was in the right places, and it matched the template closely enough that only minor tweaks were needed.

For someone who's not a designer and doesn't love fiddling with slide layouts, this was a big deal.

3. Slack + offsite logistics — without the manual headcount

This one I helped a colleague with. She was coordinating our upcoming company offsite and needed to chase up everyone who hadn't booked flights or filled out the attendance form. The problem: doing it manually meant going through every form response, cross-referencing it against the full company roster, and figuring out who was missing.

We skipped all of that. Claude CoWork connected to Slack, cross-referenced the form responses with the roster, identified everyone who hadn't responded, and drafted a personalised message to each of them — ready to send with one click.

What would have been an hour of copy-pasting and cross-referencing turned into a few minutes of setup. The messages were personalised, the list was accurate, and she didn't have to touch a spreadsheet once.

Still just scratching the surface

These are just three examples from the last few weeks. I'm actively thinking about what else I can hand off — not to cut corners, but to spend my time on the work that actually needs me.

I genuinely think AI is going to change how we work, communicate, and get things done. Not in some abstract future way — it's already happening. CoWork is one of the clearest examples I've seen of what that looks like in practice: less friction, faster output, and more time for the stuff that matters.

I'm just getting started.
