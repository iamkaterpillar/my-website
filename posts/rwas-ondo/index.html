Let's talk about Ondo Finance

Last week, I was back home visiting my parents in the Bay Area. I grew up in the heart of Silicon Valley, but despite the tech-soaked surroundings, our home is not a particularly crypto-forward household. My dad is retired and rightfully risk-averse. He's skeptical of most things blockchain-related, and understandably so. Crypto has a reputation problem.

What started as a chat about U.S. Treasury bills over coffee quickly turned into a chat about Ondo Finance. Unlike many crypto projects, Ondo isn't promising meme-driven riches or cartoon JPEGs. It's doing something far more practical, bringing real-world assets (RWAs) like U.S. Treasuries onchain.

I often learn best by writing things out. So this is my attempt to explain what Ondo is, why RWAs matter, and how this all fits into a much larger shift happening in decentralized finance.

Why I like Ondo

Despite working in crypto for the last four years, I consider myself a pretty risk-averse investor. I've been investing for over a decade — mostly in diversified ETFs like SPY (S&P 500). My crypto holdings are small, mostly BTC, ETH, and SOL, held on centralized exchanges. I only started dabbling in DeFi to better understand the landscape and test the product I helped build at Bebop.

But meme coins? Not for me.

So when I came across Ondo, I was intrigued. Here was a crypto-native product that wasn't trying to YOLO your savings into a speculative token. It was offering a blockchain-powered version of something conservative investors already love: T-bills.

The pitch: why earn 0% on USDC when you could earn 5% on USDY?

In today's uncertain economy — with inflation volatility, rising interest rates, and a returning Trump presidency — everyone is rethinking where to park their money. T-bills have long been the safest option. And now, thanks to Ondo, they're available onchain.

What Ondo offers

Ondo's value prop is simple: combine the efficiency of blockchains with the reliability of traditional assets. The company has two standout products — USDY and OUSG — both tied to U.S. Treasuries.

USDY

  • A token backed by short-term U.S. Treasuries
  • Issued under a Reg S exemption (non-U.S. holders only)
  • Accrues yield via price appreciation (starts at $1.00 and increases as interest accumulates)
  • Redeemable and tradable onchain 24/7

OUSG

  • Tokenized exposure to BlackRock's SHV ETF (short-term Treasury ETF)
  • Tradable by both U.S. and non-U.S. investors
  • Available via Coinbase Prime and other institutional platforms
  • Also gaining momentum in DeFi protocols like Flux

So... who cares about Ondo?

Well, not my dad — not yet, anyway. But a lot of others do.

Retail investors (non-U.S.)

USDY is a compelling asset for yield-hungry users around the world. In countries with inflation-prone currencies or restricted access to U.S. markets, USDY offers 5%+ yield, daily transparency, and the freedom of onchain movement.

Institutions

Ondo has drawn the attention of institutional partners by anchoring its products in familiar TradFi structures:

  • BlackRock: OUSG's underlying asset
  • Coinbase Prime: Institutional access and custody
  • DeFi integrations: Lending, collateral, and trading use cases via Flux, Mantle, Solana, and Base

DeFi protocols

USDY is being used across DeFi for lending, staking, and liquidity provision. It's programmable money, backed by real-world yield, that doesn't just sit idle — it works.

Beyond tokens: Ondo's bigger mission

Ondo isn't just issuing yield coins — it's building infrastructure. Its cross-chain bridge enables tokenized RWAs to move seamlessly across ecosystems. It's laying the groundwork for a global, composable financial system — one where U.S. Treasuries can be accessed anywhere, anytime, by anyone.

Final thoughts

We're still in the early innings of RWAs. But Ondo is showing what's possible when you combine regulatory clarity, great design, and blockchain rails. For cautious investors like my dad — and DeFi natives like me — that's something worth paying attention to.
